Spokes is a service for competitive cyclists which helps them set up and alter a training plan. The application includes extensive logic that follows coaching best practices which generates training plans for the user based on their specific training needs. Throughout each week of training the athlete can enter in each workout they complete (along with the time spent in each training zone). At the end of each week, the application acts as a ‘virtual coach,’ recommending feedback and gaining feedback from the user to enhance the quality of the training plan.

Features include:

  • Creating an account with Google
  • Ability to add, edit, or delete a workout
  • Ability to generate 15 different variations of a training plan based on a form the user fills out that goes into specifics on their training needs
  • Ability to view progress for each week
  • Ability to get feedback for completed weeks
  • Ability to get recommendations on upcoming weeks
  • Ability to input feedback which can guide you as to how you may want to alter your training plan
  • Timeline chart for FTP scores over time
  • Pie chart showing overall time spent in each zone

This application was created as my IT Innovation capstone project. The largest competitive advantage this application has over other applications involves its ability to recommend changes to the user’s training plan based on how they feel and its ability to produce variations of a training plan based on the user’s needs. This is a project I had the pleasure of working on for a year and 4 months as an intern at Union Pacific. I was tasked to migrate one of their old applications that relied on Flash (which will no longer be supported at the end of 2020) to an Angular application.

The purpose of this application is to help UP desk employees make decisions on what to do with the data that comes from detectors on the railroad tracks. These detectors include hot box detectors which measure the temperature of the axles and wheels, weight detectors which detect the weight of the wheels, and wheel profile detectors which use ultrasound technology to get the shape of the wheels. Once the data has been generated from a train passing, it will go through a prediction analysis tool which may then throw an “alert” if something seems like an outlier (e.g. a bearing is significantly hotter than the other ones on the train, the weight of a car is much higher on one side, etc.). Once an alert is produced, it will then appear in the table in the application which is constantly updating new alerts. It is then up to the UP desk employees to decide what action should be taken (e.g. set out train at next stop, repair cracked wheel, etc.) based on the alert. To help them decide, the application has many useful features allowing them to get extensive visualizations of the temperatures/weights on the train/car/axles as well as allowing them to see extensive history on the bearing/wheel temperatures and the average weight values produced by the detectors.

With the help of this application, UP can better predict when something is wrong on a train and fix it before it causes costly repairs or even train derailments.
